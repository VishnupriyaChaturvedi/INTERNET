Theorem: Every prime number (greater than 2) modulo 4 can be written in the form 4n + 1 or 4n + 3.

Any number p when divided by 4 leaves remainder 0, 1, 2, or 3. Therefore, the number has the form 4n, 4n + 1, 4n + 2, or 4n + 3. Since 4n and 4n + 2 are even, so these cannot be a form of a prime number which is an odd number (except 2). This proves the theorem. â—»
